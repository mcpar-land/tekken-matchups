How is this calculated?

Becuase certain characters have higher or lower winrates across the board, without those winrates necessarily being tied to character strength (see: Panda and Kuma having the highest winrate), these values are not based on their 'true' winrates, or else panda would just have a winning matchup against everyone, etc.

Instead, we first calculate each character's global winrate against everyone. Then, we subtract/add that winrate to the character-specific matchup winrates. Think of the numbers in this grid as "how much does character A's winrate deviate from their average winrate, when playing against character B?"

For example, if Bryan has a 55% winrate, and their value in this chart versus Shaheen is -2.5, that means their winrate versus shaheen is 55 - 2.5 = 52.5%.

Don't use these as gospel, as they have a ton of caveats:

  • Only online ranked (pulled from wavu)
  • Some characters have much larger or smaller sample sizes (Some MUs have hundreds of thousands of games played, some only hundreds)
  • Data is filtered to only include high-ish ranks (TGS/GoD and higher). Had to strike a balance between only getting "high quality" matches at high rank, with getting usable sample sizes (filtering too aggressively means some MUs are only a handful of games played)
